Developer Picks

suggested reading from the coder of this site

  • Grokking Algorithms

    This book is an insightful, beginner-friendly guide that uses illustrations and step-by-step explanations to introduce fundamental algorithms like sorting, searching, and graph traversal. It emphasizes practical applications and intuition, making complex concepts accessible even for readers without a strong math or computer science background.

    Aditya Bhargava

  • Cracking the Coding Interview, 6th Edition

    A comprehensive guide that prepares software engineers for technical interviews, this book covers data structures, algorithms, and problem-solving strategies. It includes 189 real interview questions with detailed solutions, alongside insights into the hiring process and tips for excelling in coding interviews.

    Gayle Laakmann McDowell

  • Steve Jobs

    This book (not the person it's named after!) is a biography that explores the life, career, and visionary leadership of Apple’s co-founder, detailing his relentless pursuit of innovation and perfection. Based on extensive interviews, it provides an in-depth look at his successes, failures, and impact on technology, business, and creativity.

    Walter Isaacson

  • Designing Data-Intensive Applications: The Big Ideas Behind Reliable, Scalable, and Maintainable Systems

    Want a deep dive into the architecture of modern data systems, databases, and distributed systems? This book explains how to build reliable, scalable, and maintainable applications by analyzing real-world designs and trade-offs in data storage, processing, and consistency.

    Martin Kleppman

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.

Become a member today, and make sure to check out our Staff Picks section. This week's selection is full of books anyone interested in computer programming would love!
